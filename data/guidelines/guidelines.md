# AAOS Clinical Practice Guideline: Management of Osteoarthritis of the Knee (Non-Arthroplasty)

## Indications for Total Knee Arthroplasty (TKA/TKR)
- Severe symptomatic osteoarthritis (Kellgren-Lawrence Grade III-IV)
- Failure of non-surgical treatment for at least three months.
- Non-surgical treatments include:
    - Physical therapy (strengthening and aerobic exercises)
    - Oral medications (NSAIDs, acetaminophen)
    - Intra-articular injections (corticosteroids)
    - Weight loss (for BMI >25)
- Clear radiographic joint space narrowing, osteophytes, or subchondral sclerosis.
- Functional impairment significantly impacting activities of daily living (ADLs).

## Documentation Requirements
- Detailed history of conservative treatments including duration and outcome.
- Radiographic reports (X-ray, MRI, or CT).
- Physical exam findings (range of motion, instability, malalignment).

# CMS National Coverage Determination (NCD) 150.6: Total Knee Replacement
- Coverage provided when the procedure is performed for symptomatic arthritis of the knee.
- Requires documentation that clinical management has failed to provide relief.
- Conservative management should be documented and sustained for at least 3 months.
